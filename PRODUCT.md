# Product

## Register

product

## Users

- **Venue back-office (primary):** café/restaurant **owners & managers** (analytics, menu, staff, subscription), **cashiers** (fast POS under a queue), **kitchen staff** (KDS under heat/noise/pressure). They work on tablets and small screens, standing, often one-handed, in bright or cramped environments — speed and legibility beat decoration.
- **Diners (public):** anonymous guests scanning a QR to browse the menu and order — no login, phone-first, RTL Arabic.
- **Platform admins (us):** the company operating the SaaS — a separate, visually isolated console overseeing all venues.

## Product Purpose

A multi-tenant QR-ordering + full restaurant-management SaaS (MenuLink / نيما): digital menu, orders, cashier/POS, kitchen display (KDS), tables, staff & attendance, inventory & recipes, loyalty/VIP, offers, analytics — plus a central platform console (subscriptions, support, oversight). Success = a venue runs its whole floor from one fast, trustworthy Arabic system, and the platform owner controls every venue from one place. Monetized via tiered packages (menu → operations → pro → enterprise).

## Brand Personality

Professional, fast, trustworthy, calm-under-pressure. Three words: **operational, confident, precise.** It should feel like dependable equipment a busy team reaches for a hundred times a shift — not a playful consumer app, not a heavy enterprise dashboard. Arabic-first, RTL-native.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS-cream / purple-blue "AI gradient" dashboards.
- Cluttered legacy POS screens (tiny buttons, dense noise, no hierarchy).
- Toy-like, over-rounded, over-animated café apps.
- Anything that slows a cashier or a kitchen line down for the sake of looking pretty.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed under pressure.** POS and KDS must be instant, glanceable, and reachable with a thumb. Every extra tap or ambiguous state costs the venue money.
2. **Density with clarity.** Data-dense is fine (orders, prices, stock); clutter is not. Strong hierarchy, tabular numbers, one clear action per context.
3. **RTL-native, bilingual.** Arabic is the default reality, not an afterthought — logical properties, mirrored flows, legible Arabic type.
4. **Calm, not loud.** Confident quiet over decorative shouting. Color carries meaning (order states, alerts), not vibe.
5. **Tiered polish.** The system offers selectable **templates per section** (cashier, kitchen, dashboard…) gated by plan — the same engine, different fit for different venues.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target: body text ≥ 4.5:1, large/state text ≥ 3:1 — critical in bright kitchens and on cheap tablets. Touch targets ≥ 44px (POS/KDS often larger). Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support. Color is never the only signal (pair with label/icon) for order states. Arabic + English legibility with tabular figures for all data.
